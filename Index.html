Pearl Database Overview

Pearl is our centralized learning management system for session scheduling, attendance tracking, and quality monitoring. It serves as the foundation for all tutoring operations data.

Core Data Collection Points

Instructor Attendance Sessions attended, missed, late, and cancellation tracking
Student Attendance Scholar participation, absences, and patterns
Instructor Surveys Post-session quality reports with engagement ratings
Student Surveys Scholar feedback on learning and enjoyment
Session Details Scheduled vs. actual times, subjects, grades
Attendance Detail Join/leave times, late arrivals, missed reasons

Pearl Report Types

Instructor/Student Attendance Summary

  • User: Instructor/Student Name
  • Attended Sessions: # of sessions marked Attended
  • Attended Time: HH:MM:SS of attended sessions
  • Missed Sessions: # of sessions marked Missed
  • Late: # of sessions marked Late
  • % Attended: Attendance percentage calculation
  • Canceled Sessions: # of assigned sessions marked Canceled

Additional Fields: Sex, School, Region, District, Custom ID

Granular Session-Level Data

  • User name, email/username, role
  • Session title/details and status
  • Planned vs. Actual session start/end times
  • First join and last leave times
  • Attendance Status:
    • All (includes all statuses)
    • Attended (marked present)
    • Late (marked late)
    • Missed (did not attend)
    • Subbed (substituted via QuickSub)
    • Not Recorded (attendance not marked)
  • Demographics: Grade, Sex, Race, Ethnicity, School, Region, District

Instructor Post Session Survey

  • How engaged was the scholar? (1-5 scale)
  • How much did you enjoy this session? (1-5 scale)
  • How much do you think the scholar learned? (1-5 scale)
  • Overall, how did this tutoring session go? (1-5 scale)
  • Comments for student (visible to parents)
  • Admin flag: "Do you need an administrator to look into this session?"
  • Comments for Admin (private)
  • Comments for yourself (private notes)

Metadata: Time to complete, session date/length, scheduled vs. actual times, Pearl IDs

Student Post Session Survey

  • How confident are you that you understood the material? (1-5 scale)
  • How much did you enjoy this session? (1-5 scale)
  • How much do you think you learned? (1-5 scale)
  • Overall, how did this tutoring session go? (1-5 scale)
  • Comments (optional)

Metadata: Time to complete, session date/length, scheduled vs. actual times, Pearl IDs

Collection Points by Role

During Each Session

  • Mark attendance for all scholars (Attended, Late, Missed)
  • Complete Instructor Post Session Survey immediately after session ends
  • Flag any issues requiring admin attention
  • Guide scholars in completing their post-session surveys

Survey Completion Guidelines

  • When: Within 24 hours of session completion
  • Where: Pearl Dashboard ‚Üí Sessions ‚Üí View Session ‚Üí Enter Survey
  • Required: All rating questions (1-5 scale)
  • Optional but Encouraged: Comments for context
üìå Critical: For in-person and alternative URL sessions (Zoom, Google Meet), attendance must be manually marked. Pearl Online Classroom sessions auto-log attendance.

After Each Session

  • Complete Student Post Session Survey with instructor guidance
  • Provide honest feedback on understanding, enjoyment, and learning
  • Add optional comments if desired

Survey Timing

Surveys should be completed during the session timeframe or immediately after. Surveys completed outside the session window are flagged as "out of range."

üìä Current Status: 35% of scholar surveys are being completed outside the session timeframe, indicating need for reinforcement of real-time completion practices.

Daily Monitoring

  • Review incomplete sessions and follow up with instructors
  • Monitor survey completion rates
  • Address any red flags raised in instructor surveys
  • Coordinate substitutions and schedule adjustments

Weekly Tasks

  • Review attendance patterns and identify chronic absenteeism
  • Communicate with families about attendance concerns
  • Report service interruptions (testing, school closures, etc.)
  • Provide programmatic context for data team

Data Collection Standards

Completeness Requirements

  • Attendance: Must be marked for 100% of scheduled sessions
  • Instructor Surveys: Target 90% completion rate
  • Scholar Surveys: Target 80% completion rate
  • Session Details: Subject and grade level required for all completed sessions
  • Location Data: School/District field required for all rostered staff and scholars

Data Quality Standards

  • Surveys completed within 24 hours of session
  • Attendance marked accurately (not "Not Recorded")
  • Comments provided when flagging issues
  • Service interruptions documented with reason codes
‚úÖ Best Practice: Set aside the last 5 minutes of each session for scholars to complete surveys on their devices while memories are fresh.

Flag Types & Thresholds

Definition: 20% or more of all sessions assigned to a tutor are marked as incomplete

Calculation: (Incomplete Sessions / Total Assigned Sessions) √ó 100

What "Incomplete" Means

A session is marked incomplete when:

  • Attendance has not been finalized
  • Session end time was not confirmed
  • Required post-session actions were not completed

Implications

  • Data gaps prevent accurate attendance tracking and reporting
  • May indicate technical difficulties accessing Pearl
  • Could suggest need for additional training on session finalization
  • Impacts ability to generate complete reports for stakeholders

Action Protocol

  1. Site coordinator contacts tutor within 48 hours
  2. Identify root cause: technical issue, training need, or workflow challenge
  3. Provide targeted support based on cause
  4. Monitor weekly for 2-3 weeks to ensure improvement
  5. If unresolved after 3 weeks, escalate to Regional Manager
üìä Example Scenario: A tutor with 25 assigned sessions has 6 incomplete sessions. This equals 24% incomplete rate, triggering a high incomplete flag.

Adaptive Definition: Two weeks' worth of consecutive direct instruction missed, adjusted for program schedule

How It Works

The chronic absenteeism flag adapts based on how many days per week a program operates:

  • Programs meeting 2x/week (e.g., GLAW): 4 consecutive missed sessions = chronic absenteeism flag
  • Programs meeting 3x/week: 6 consecutive missed sessions = chronic absenteeism flag
  • Programs meeting 5x/week: 10 consecutive missed sessions = chronic absenteeism flag

Rationale

This approach allows us to effectively flag all of our programs, regardless of how many days per week sessions are held. The focus is on two weeks of consecutive missed instruction, not a fixed number of days.

Implication

  • Risk of disengagement and learning loss
  • Family may need re-connection or schedule adjustment
  • Possible external barriers (transportation, health, family circumstances)

Action Protocol

  1. Site coordinator reaches out to family within 24 hours of flag
  2. Document reason for absences and family communication
  3. Weekly updates shared with regional managers (by Monday EOD)
  4. If no contact after 2 attempts, escalate to District POC
  5. Continue monitoring for 2 weeks post-intervention

Example Scenario: A school program operates twice per week. After a scholar misses 4 consecutive sessions (2 weeks of instruction), the chronic absenteeism flag triggers. The site coordinator reaches out to the family to understand barriers and offer support. After re-engagement efforts and schedule adjustments, the scholar's attendance improves over the following weeks.

Ticket Log System

The SY 25-26 Program Ticket Log is the central tracking mechanism for all flagged issues.

Ticket Log Structure

  • Timestamp: When issue was identified
  • Submitted By: Data team member flagging issue
  • Associated Region: North-East or South-West
  • Site/Program: Specific location
  • Flagged Indicator: Type of concern (attendance, survey, quality, behavior)
  • Flagged For: Tutor name, Scholar name, or District
  • Issue Description: Detailed explanation of the concern
  • Immediate Context: Any known background information
  • Programmatic Action Required: Recommended next steps
  • Action Status: Weekly tracking columns (Ongoing, Escalation Required, Resolved)
  • Actual Resolve Date: Date issue was closed
  • Notes: Running log of interventions and outcomes

Status Definitions

  • Ongoing: Issue acknowledged, monitoring in progress, no immediate escalation needed
  • Escalation Required: Threshold crossed, program team action needed
  • Resolved: Issue addressed, outcome documented, no further monitoring needed
  • N/A: Not applicable or pre-dates tracking period
‚úÖ Best Practice: Update ticket statuses weekly during dashboard refresh cycle to maintain accurate tracking and accountability.

Policy Application Examples

Initial Concern: Scholar enjoyment ratings consistently below benchmark (2.0-2.5/5), learning ratings at 3.0/5

Investigation: One scholar providing consistently low ratings; site leader observation revealed comprehension challenges

Root Cause: Scholar struggled with academic vocabulary and may have misunderstood survey questions

Intervention:

  • Tutor trained to review survey questions with scholars before completion
  • Site leader offered to support scholar during future surveys
  • Additional one-on-one time provided for relationship building

Outcome: Ratings improved progressively from 2.0 ‚Üí 2.7 ‚Üí 3.0 ‚Üí 3.5 ‚Üí 3.7 over time

Lesson Learned: Low ratings don't always indicate poor performance; sometimes indicate need for scaffolding or clarification

Initial Concern: School-wide scholar attendance at 36% during early program weeks

Investigation: Contextual factors identified - diagnostic testing period, transition to formal tutoring

Intervention:

  • School point of contact engaged staff to contact all tutored families
  • Parents confirmed continued participation interest
  • Individual scholars with chronic absences flagged for targeted outreach
  • Testing period concluded, regular schedule established

Outcome: Attendance improved from 36% to 46% as routines stabilized

Lesson Learned: Always seek programmatic context before escalating; early-year trends may reflect adjustment periods

Initial Concern: 25+ flagged behavior comments across multiple tutors in one district

Pattern Analysis:

  • Several comments noted technology disruptions (device failures)
  • Some referenced scholar disengagement during transition times
  • Few indicated high energy but productive sessions

Systemic Intervention:

  • Behavioral Tracking Sheet created for status monitoring
  • School climate issue identified: Equipment storage too far from tutoring spaces
  • Engagement strategies resource shared with all tutors
  • Weekly status update protocol implemented

Outcome: Majority of concerns resolved through equipment logistics and coaching; clear response system established

Lesson Learned: Multiple individual flags can reveal systemic issues requiring operational solutions, not just individual coaching

Initial Concern: Tutor consistently had no scholar surveys recorded despite attended sessions

Investigation: Recent scholar-tutor interaction escalated to admin; surveys stopped being completed

Root Cause: Tutor acknowledged time management challenge: "time escapes us... slow movements"

Intervention:

  • Admin meeting held to address scholar interaction and rapport-building
  • Tutor committed to having scholars complete surveys before dismissal
  • Site coordinator provided tips on time management and session pacing

Outcome: Scholar surveys resumed for all subsequent sessions; issue resolved

Lesson Learned: Survey completion issues can indicate deeper classroom management or relationship challenges requiring holistic support

Data Governance Framework

This section outlines the policies, procedures, and standards that ensure data integrity, security, and appropriate use across NJTC operations.

üõ°Ô∏è Core Principle: Data is the foundation of decision-making. Our governance structure protects data integrity, accuracy, privacy, and intellectual property.

Authority & Ownership

Centralized Data Authority

Director of Program Evaluation & Impact is the sole authority for:

  • All data systems, dashboards, and analytics workflows
  • Data pulling, manipulation, and transformation
  • Dashboard creation and updates
  • Benchmark setting and modification
  • Reporting structures and cadences

What This Means

  • ‚ùå No other staff member handles, manipulates, or updates raw or transformed data
  • ‚ùå No copying of proprietary dashboards, automation, or workflows
  • ‚ùå No alterations to dashboard logic or calculations by non-authorized personnel
  • ‚úÖ All data requests route through the Director
  • ‚úÖ Context and interpretation provided by regional teams, but not raw data access
Rationale: Single point of authority prevents inconsistencies, errors, and unauthorized modifications. Ensures all stakeholders work from the same source of truth.

Scope & Frequency

Monitoring Categories

  • Scholar Attendance: Overall rates and individual patterns
  • Tutor Delivery: Attendance, session execution, survey completion
  • Scholar Experience: Surveys on learning, enjoyment, engagement
  • Instructor Reflection: Quality culture inputs, session notes

Reporting Cadence

Frequency Activity
Weekly Pull attendance, survey, and session data; internal QA review; dashboard updates
Monthly Trend review and leadership briefing
Quarterly Formal progress reporting aligned to diagnostic cycles
Ad-Hoc If critical operational variance or partner request

Privacy & Access Requirements

Data Minimization

  • Scholar and tutor IDs only in reporting and analysis
  • Names used only for operational follow-up when necessary
  • Access restricted based on role; minimum necessary principle
  • Reports shared in secure formats (PDF lock, controlled permissions)

Access Tiers

  • Level 1 - Director PEI: Full access to all raw and transformed data
  • Level 2 - Regional Managers: Dashboard access for their region, cannot export raw data
  • Level 3 - Site Coordinators: Dashboard access for their site, ticketing system for context
  • Level 4 - Tutors: Access to their own past sessions, surveys, and attendance only
  • Level 5 - Partners: Aggregated, anonymized dashboards for their school/district
üîí Reminder: No raw files or dashboards are to be copied, re-created, or altered by other parties. Proprietary logic and workflows are confidential.

Escalation Protocol

When data flags operational concerns, follow this escalation pathway:

1

Verification

Data team conducts internal cross-checks to confirm flag validity

2

Notification

Inform program leadership (Regional Manager, Director of Programs)

3

Communication

Site leader(s) contacted if intervention required

4

Documentation

Log resolution pathway in ticket log

5

Follow-Up Monitoring

Continue tracking for recurrence, update dashboard benchmarks if needed

Important: This SOP supports improvement, not punitive action. Data insights guide coaching, support, and systemic improvements.

Continuity Planning

In the Director's Absence

  • Only a static operations report may be issued (pre-approved template)
  • No live environment updates or backend changes are permitted
  • Access permissions remain unchanged
  • Urgent issues escalate to Executive Leadership

All team members must adhere to:

  • Confidentiality expectations (no sharing of identifiable data)
  • Standard reporting cadence (weekly/monthly/quarterly)
  • Access boundaries (only view data relevant to your role)
  • Escalation pathways (follow proper channels for data requests)
